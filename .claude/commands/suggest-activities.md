Read `5-remember.md` in the current directory and add self-study activities throughout the content. Write the result to `6-activities.md`.

These are activities the reader does on their own, applying concepts to their own content or learners.

## What to do

Include at least one activity or reflection exercise per section. Place them where they are most useful — often right after introducing a key concept. They should feel like a natural part of the content flow, not bolted on at the end.

## Activity format

```markdown
### Activity: [Title]

[Clear instructions for the activity. Tell the reader exactly what to do — write something down, discuss with a partner, review their own materials, try a technique. Keep it grounded in their own training context. The activity should help them apply the concept from the section to their own work.]
```

## Guidelines

- Use the **voice-and-tone** skill for tone
- Activities should be practical and grounded in the reader's own training context
- Tell the reader exactly what to do — be specific, not vague
- Vary the types: writing exercises, reflection prompts, reviewing their own materials, trying a technique, comparing approaches
- Keep instructions concise — a short paragraph is usually enough
- Use examples liberally in surrounding content. When explaining a concept, show what it looks like in a real training setting
- Do not remove or modify existing content — only add activities
