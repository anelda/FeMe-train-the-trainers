# Outcomes Map

## Outcomes → Exercises

### Outcome 1: Apply a growth mindset to your own development as an educator, not just to your learners.
- **Bloom's level:** Apply (verb: *apply*)
- **Exercise:** Activity: Where does your mindset sit? — Bloom's level: Analyse

### Outcome 2: Evaluate why your current feedback sources may be too slow or too noisy to effectively guide your teaching.
- **Bloom's level:** Evaluate (verb: *evaluate*)
- **Exercise:** Activity: Map your feedback sources — Bloom's level: Evaluate

### Outcome 3: Design a low-stakes check on understanding that creates a faster feedback loop for both you and your learners.
- **Bloom's level:** Create (verb: *design*)
- **Exercise:** Activity: Design a quick check — Bloom's level: Create

### Outcome 4: Analyse how the curse of knowledge shapes your own teaching practice.
- **Bloom's level:** Analyse (verb: *analyse*)
- **Exercise:** Activity: Spot the curse of knowledge — Bloom's level: Analyse

### Outcome 5: Design a simple diagnostic approach to uncover where a struggling learner's understanding breaks down before choosing an intervention.
- **Bloom's level:** Create (verb: *design*)
- **Exercise:** Activity: Walk through a diagnosis — Bloom's level: Create

## Exercises → Outcomes

### Exercise: Activity: Where does your mindset sit?
- **Bloom's level:** Analyse (learners examine and classify their mindset across skills, identify shifts)
- **Serves outcome:** "Apply a growth mindset to your own development..." — Outcome Bloom's level: Apply

### Exercise: Activity: Spot the curse of knowledge
- **Bloom's level:** Analyse (learners identify assumptions and jargon a newcomer would find confusing)
- **Serves outcome:** "Analyse how the curse of knowledge shapes your own teaching practice." — Outcome Bloom's level: Analyse

### Exercise: Activity: Map your feedback sources
- **Bloom's level:** Evaluate (learners assess speed and specificity of their feedback sources)
- **Serves outcome:** "Evaluate why your current feedback sources may be too slow or too noisy..." — Outcome Bloom's level: Evaluate

### Exercise: Activity: Design a quick check
- **Bloom's level:** Create (learners produce a question and plan a response)
- **Serves outcome:** "Design a low-stakes check on understanding..." — Outcome Bloom's level: Create

### Exercise: Activity: Walk through a diagnosis
- **Bloom's level:** Create (learners break down a concept, write diagnostic questions, plan an intervention sequence)
- **Serves outcome:** "Design a simple diagnostic approach..." — Outcome Bloom's level: Create

### Reflection Q1: Which of the mindsets discussed in this chapter are useful for learners?
- **Bloom's level:** Evaluate
- **⚠️ ORPHAN** — this reflection does not clearly serve any listed outcome.

### Reflection Q2: How could you encourage your learners to self-assess their own understanding and actively seek out knowledge they are missing?
- **Bloom's level:** Apply
- **Serves outcome:** Loosely bridges to later sessions. No direct outcome match in this chapter.

## Bloom's Alignment Issues

- **⚠️ EXERCISE EXCEEDS OUTCOME** — "Where does your mindset sit?" (Analyse) exceeds Outcome 1 (Apply). The exercise asks learners to examine, classify, and plan — not just apply. Suggestion: level up the outcome verb from "Apply" to "Analyse", e.g.: "Analyse where your own mindset sits across different teaching skills and plan concrete steps to shift towards growth."

## Flags for Review

- **Flag 1: Exercise exceeds Outcome 1.** "Where does your mindset sit?" operates at Analyse but Outcome 1 targets Apply. Suggestion: level up the outcome verb.
- **Flag 2: Reflection Q1 is unanchored.** "Which of the mindsets discussed in this chapter are useful for learners?" does not map to any listed outcome. Acceptable as a closing reflection, or add an outcome like "Evaluate which educator mindsets are also valuable for learners."
- **No vague verbs.** All outcome verbs are clear and Bloom's-aligned.
- **No unmet outcomes.** Every outcome has at least one well-matched exercise.
