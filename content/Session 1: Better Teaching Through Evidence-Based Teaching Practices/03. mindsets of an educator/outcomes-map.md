# Outcomes Map

## Outcomes → Exercises

### Outcome 1: Explain how a growth mindset applies to your own development as an educator, not just to your learners.
- **Bloom's level:** Understand (verb: explain)
- **Exercise:** "Where does your mindset sit?" — Bloom's level: Apply (learners apply the growth/fixed mindset framework to their own teaching skills and plan a concrete shift)

### Outcome 2: Identify why common feedback sources (such as end-of-term results) are often too slow or too noisy to guide your teaching.
- **Bloom's level:** Remember (verb: identify)
- **Exercise:** "Map your feedback sources" — Bloom's level: Analyse (learners list their feedback sources and then evaluate each one against speed and specificity criteria)

### Outcome 3: Describe how frequent, low-stakes checks on understanding create a faster feedback loop for both you and your learners.
- **Bloom's level:** Remember (verb: describe)
- **Exercise:** "Design a quick check" — Bloom's level: Create (learners design a check question and plan a response strategy)

### Outcome 4: Recognise the role of the curse of knowledge and illusions of competence in your own teaching practice.
- **Bloom's level:** Remember (verb: recognise)
- **Exercise:** "Spot the curse of knowledge" — Bloom's level: Analyse (learners analyse their own expertise to identify likely stumbling points for newcomers)

### Outcome 5: Design a simple diagnostic approach to uncover where a struggling learner's understanding breaks down before choosing an intervention.
- **Bloom's level:** Create (verb: design)
- **Exercise:** "Walk through a diagnosis" — Bloom's level: Create (learners break down a concept into building blocks, write diagnostic questions, and plan an intervention sequence)

## Bloom's Alignment Issues

### Outcome 1 ↔ Exercise "Where does your mindset sit?"
- Outcome: Understand (explain) | Exercise: Apply
- **⚠️ EXERCISE EXCEEDS OUTCOME** — The exercise asks learners to apply the concept to their own practice and plan a change, which goes beyond explaining. Suggestion: level up the outcome verb to "Apply" (e.g., "Apply a growth mindset to your own development as an educator") or keep it as-is and accept that the exercise deliberately stretches learners beyond the stated outcome.

### Outcome 2 ↔ Exercise "Map your feedback sources"
- Outcome: Remember (identify) | Exercise: Analyse
- **⚠️ EXERCISE EXCEEDS OUTCOME** — The exercise asks learners to evaluate their feedback sources against criteria, which is substantially more demanding than merely identifying why feedback sources are slow or noisy. Suggestion: level up the outcome verb to "Analyse" or "Evaluate" (e.g., "Evaluate why your current feedback sources may be too slow or too noisy to guide your teaching").

### Outcome 3 ↔ Exercise "Design a quick check"
- Outcome: Remember (describe) | Exercise: Create
- **⚠️ EXERCISE EXCEEDS OUTCOME** — This is the largest mismatch. The outcome only asks learners to describe a concept, but the exercise asks them to create an original check question and plan their response. Suggestion: level up the outcome verb to "Design" or "Create" (e.g., "Design a low-stakes check on understanding that serves as a feedback loop for both you and your learners").

### Outcome 4 ↔ Exercise "Spot the curse of knowledge"
- Outcome: Remember (recognise) | Exercise: Analyse
- **⚠️ EXERCISE EXCEEDS OUTCOME** — The exercise asks learners to analyse their own expertise and surface hidden assumptions, which goes well beyond recognising that these biases exist. Suggestion: level up the outcome verb to "Analyse" (e.g., "Analyse how the curse of knowledge and illusions of competence affect your own teaching practice").

### Outcome 5 ↔ Exercise "Walk through a diagnosis"
- Outcome: Create (design) | Exercise: Create
- **✅ ALIGNED** — Both the outcome and the exercise operate at Create level.

## Flags for Review

### Bloom's level mismatches (4 of 5 outcomes)

1. **Outcome 1** (Understand) vs exercise (Apply) — mild mismatch. Suggested fix: change "Explain" to "Apply" → *"Apply a growth mindset to your own development as an educator, not just to your learners."*

2. **Outcome 2** (Remember) vs exercise (Analyse) — significant mismatch. Suggested fix: change "Identify" to "Evaluate" → *"Evaluate why your current feedback sources may be too slow or too noisy to effectively guide your teaching."*

3. **Outcome 3** (Remember) vs exercise (Create) — largest mismatch. Suggested fix: change "Describe" to "Design" → *"Design a low-stakes check on understanding that creates a faster feedback loop for both you and your learners."*

4. **Outcome 4** (Remember) vs exercise (Analyse) — significant mismatch. Suggested fix: change "Recognise" to "Analyse" → *"Analyse how the curse of knowledge and illusions of competence shape your own teaching practice."*

### Partial orphan

5. **Reflection question 2** ("How might the learners in your course adopt the 'taking personal responsibility' mindset?") — The "taking personal responsibility" theme runs through the prose but has no dedicated outcome. Options:
   - Add an outcome (e.g., "Explain why treating learner struggles as diagnostic challenges — rather than writing them off — improves both your teaching and the learner's experience.")
   - Accept that the reflection deliberately goes beyond the stated outcomes as a stretch prompt.
   - Reword the reflection to tie more directly to outcome 5 (the diagnostic approach).

### No unmet outcomes

All five outcomes have at least one exercise addressing them.

### No fully orphan exercises

All exercises serve at least one outcome, though the reflection questions serve them loosely.
