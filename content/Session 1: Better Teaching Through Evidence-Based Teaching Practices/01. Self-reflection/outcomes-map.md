## Flags for Review

### Flag 1: Only one learning outcome for the entire section
The prose explicitly states three additional goals in "Why we start with reflection":
- Surface assumptions that shape teaching (Analyse)
- Clarify the change you want to produce in learners (Analyse)
- Examine whether methods align with goals (Evaluate)

These are not captured as formal outcomes, yet the opening self-reflection exercise directly addresses them.

**Suggestion:** Add 1–2 outcomes, for example:
- "Examine how your assumptions about learning influence the way you design teaching." (Analyse)
- "Assess whether your current teaching methods align with the learning goals you have for your participants." (Evaluate)

Or keep the single outcome and treat the opening reflection purely as scaffolding.

### Flag 2: Opening self-reflection weakly matches the stated outcome
The opening reflection does not ask learners to *design* anything. It only serves the outcome indirectly as preparation. If no additional outcomes are added, it is a near-orphan.

### Flag 3: No vague verbs
The outcome verb "design" is specific and clearly at the Create level. No changes needed.
