# Research: Open Educational Resources (OER)

## Claim 1: Definition of OER

**Claim:** OER are "freely available teaching materials that you can use, adapt, and redistribute."

**Verdict: Essentially correct, but simplified.**

The official UNESCO definition (from the 2019 Recommendation on OER) states:

> Open Educational Resources are "learning, teaching and research materials in any format and medium that reside in the public domain or are under copyright that have been released under an open license, that permit no-cost access, re-use, re-purpose, adaptation and redistribution by others."

The workbook's phrasing captures the core idea accurately. The full definition adds nuance:

- OER cover **learning, teaching, and research** materials (not just teaching materials).
- They must be in the **public domain** or released under an **open license**.
- The permissions include not just use, adaptation, and redistribution, but also **re-purposing**.

For the workbook's audience, the simplified definition is appropriate and not misleading. A brief mention of open licensing would strengthen it.

## Claim 2: OER Commons exists as a repository of open educational resources

**Verdict: Correct.**

OER Commons (<https://oercommons.org/>) is a public digital library of open educational resources created by the Institute for the Study of Knowledge Management in Education (ISKME). It hosts over 50,000 OER and allows educators to search, browse, evaluate, create, and share open materials. The site is active and maintained as of 2026.

### Sources

- OER Commons: <https://oercommons.org/>
- Wikipedia — OER Commons: <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OER_Commons>

---

## Claim 3: MIT OpenCourseWare exists as a repository of open educational resources

**Verdict: Correct.**

MIT OpenCourseWare (<https://ocw.mit.edu/>) is a permanent MIT initiative that publishes materials from more than 2,500 MIT courses under a Creative Commons license. It requires no registration or enrolment. The site is actively maintained, with new content and news articles published as recently as February and March 2026.

### Sources

- MIT OpenCourseWare: <https://ocw.mit.edu/>
- MIT OpenCourseWare — About: <https://ocw.mit.edu/about/>
- MIT News (Feb 2026): <https://news.mit.edu/2026/how-mit-opencourseware-fueling-one-learners-passion-for-education-0218>

---

## Summary

All three claims are accurate. The definition of OER used in the workbook is a fair simplification of the UNESCO standard. Both OER Commons and MIT OpenCourseWare are real, active, and well-established repositories of openly licensed educational materials.
