# Workshop Activity Alignment Report

## Activity 1: Backward Design Speed Round
- **What participants do:** Write an outcome statement, then a partner designs an assessment and activity for it; pairs compare interpretations.
- **Activity Bloom's level:** Apply
- **Matched outcome:** Outcome 1 — Apply the backward design process to plan a focused training session, and explain why it produces more focused training than starting with content.
- **Outcome Bloom's level:** Apply / Understand
- **Alignment:** MATCH

## Activity 2: The Verb Upgrade
- **What participants do:** Rewrite vague learning objectives to be observable, measurable, and pitched at the right thinking level.
- **Activity Bloom's level:** Create
- **Matched outcome:** Outcome 2 — Write learning objectives that are observable, measurable, and pitched at the right level of thinking.
- **Outcome Bloom's level:** Create
- **Alignment:** MATCH

## Activity 3: Alignment Audit
- **What participants do:** Map objectives, activities, and assessments in a real training session; identify gaps, extras, and mismatches; propose concrete fixes.
- **Activity Bloom's level:** Analyse / Evaluate
- **Matched outcome:** Outcome 3 — Identify misalignment between objectives, activities, and assessments in a training plan.
- **Outcome Bloom's level:** Analyse
- **Alignment:** ACTIVITY EXCEEDS OUTCOME

The activity asks participants to both identify misalignment and propose fixes, which pushes into Evaluate. The exceed is one level and appropriate for a facilitated workshop. The outcomes map already flags this pattern for the self-study exercise "Spot the misalignment." If Outcome 3's verb is later levelled up from "identify" to "evaluate," alignment becomes exact.

## Activity 4: Sequence Scramble
- **What participants do:** Arrange topic cards into a teaching sequence based on dependencies, then redesign part of the sequence using productive failure.
- **Activity Bloom's level:** Analyse / Evaluate
- **Matched outcome:** Outcome 4 — Analyse the dependencies between topics in a training session to sequence content effectively, and evaluate when productive struggle is more effective than gradual progression.
- **Outcome Bloom's level:** Analyse / Evaluate
- **Alignment:** MATCH

## Activity 5: The Cutting Room Floor
- **What participants do:** Identify content in their own session that does not serve a learning objective, then a partner recommends keep, cut, or move.
- **Activity Bloom's level:** Evaluate
- **Matched outcome:** Outcome 6 — Evaluate whether a session's content is focused enough to support real learning, and remove what does not serve the objectives.
- **Outcome Bloom's level:** Evaluate / Apply
- **Alignment:** MATCH

## Activity 6: Pitfall Clinic
- **What participants do:** Identify which common design pitfall they are most guilty of, describe a specific instance, brainstorm a concrete change, and evaluate whether that change genuinely serves their session's objectives.
- **Activity Bloom's level:** Evaluate
- **Matched outcome:** Outcome 6 — Evaluate whether a session's content is focused enough to support real learning, and remove what does not serve the objectives.
- **Outcome Bloom's level:** Evaluate
- **Alignment:** MATCH

The added evaluation step — testing the proposed fix against the session's objectives — raises the activity from Analyse/Apply to Evaluate, aligning it with Outcome 6.

## Unmatched outcomes

- **Outcome 5:** Evaluate an existing open educational resource for alignment with your learning objectives and learner level. No workshop activity covers this. The self-study exercise "Find and evaluate one resource" handles it. OER evaluation requires browsing and comparing resources, which is better suited to self-study than a timed workshop activity.

## Summary

- 5 activities aligned (Activities 1, 2, 4, 5, 6)
- 1 activity exceeds its outcome by one level (Activity 3 — acceptable for workshop setting)
- 0 activities undershoot their outcome
- 0 activities with no matching outcome
- 1 outcome with no workshop activity (Outcome 5 — covered by self-study)
