# Outcomes Map

## Outcomes → Exercises

### Outcome 1: Apply the backward design process to plan a focused training session, and explain why it produces more focused training than starting with content.
- **Bloom's level:** Apply (verb: *apply*) / Understand (verb: *explain*)
- **Exercise:** Activity: Design backwards from one goal — Bloom's level: Apply
- **Exercise:** Activity: Map your activities to your objectives — Bloom's level: Analyse (also serves Outcome 3)

### Outcome 2: Write learning objectives that are observable, measurable, and pitched at the right level of thinking.
- **Bloom's level:** Create (verb: *write*)
- **Exercise:** Activity: Rewrite your own objectives — Bloom's level: Create

### Outcome 3: Identify misalignment between objectives, activities, and assessments in a training plan.
- **Bloom's level:** Analyse (verb: *identify*)
- **Exercise:** Activity: Spot the misalignment — Bloom's level: Evaluate
- **Exercise:** Activity: Map your activities to your objectives — Bloom's level: Analyse

### Outcome 4: Analyse the dependencies between topics in a training session to sequence content effectively, and evaluate when productive struggle is more effective than gradual progression.
- **Bloom's level:** Analyse (verb: *analyse*) / Evaluate (verb: *evaluate*)
- **Exercise:** Activity: Draw a dependency map — Bloom's level: Analyse
- **Exercise:** Activity: Gradual build-up or productive failure? — Bloom's level: Evaluate

### Outcome 5: Evaluate an existing open educational resource for alignment with your learning objectives and learner level.
- **Bloom's level:** Evaluate (verb: *evaluate*)
- **Exercise:** Activity: Find and evaluate one resource — Bloom's level: Evaluate

### Outcome 6: Evaluate whether a session's content is focused enough to support real learning, and remove what does not serve the objectives.
- **Bloom's level:** Evaluate (verb: *evaluate*) / Apply (verb: *remove*)
- **Exercise:** Activity: The cutting exercise — Bloom's level: Evaluate
- **Exercise:** Activity: Review your own design for pitfalls — Bloom's level: Evaluate

## Bloom's Alignment Issues

- **⚠️ EXERCISE EXCEEDS OUTCOME** — "Spot the misalignment" (Evaluate) exceeds Outcome 3 (Analyse). The exercise asks learners to evaluate alignment and propose changes, not just identify misalignment. Suggestion: level up the outcome verb from "identify" to "evaluate."

## Flags for Review

- **Flag 1 (main): ORPHAN exercise — "Write three questions to find out what your learners know."** No outcome covers needs assessment. Suggestion: add a new outcome, e.g. "Design questions to assess what your learners already know before you begin training."
- **Flag 2: Exercise exceeds Outcome 3.** "Spot the misalignment" operates at Evaluate but Outcome 3 targets Analyse. Suggestion: level up the outcome verb from "identify" to "evaluate."
- **Flag 3 (minor): "Map your activities to your objectives" exceeds Outcome 1.** The exercise operates at Analyse while Outcome 1 targets Apply. Acceptable — the exercise bridges Outcomes 1 and 3.
- **Flag 4 (informational): Compound outcomes.** Outcomes 1, 4, and 6 each contain two verbs at different Bloom's levels. Not necessarily wrong — the parts are closely related. Optional to split.
- **No vague verbs.** All outcome verbs are clear and Bloom's-aligned.
- **No unmet outcomes.** Every outcome has at least one well-matched exercise.
