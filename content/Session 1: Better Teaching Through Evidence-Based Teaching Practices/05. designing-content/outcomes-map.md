## Bloom's Alignment Issues

### Outcome 1 (Understand) ← Exercise: Design backwards from one goal (Apply)
- **⚠️ EXERCISE EXCEEDS OUTCOME** — The exercise asks learners to *apply* the backward design process to their own session, but the outcome only requires them to *explain* the process. **Suggestion:** Level up the outcome verb from "explain" to "apply" (e.g., "Apply the backward design process to plan a focused training session"), or keep the outcome at "explain" and accept that the exercise deliberately stretches beyond it as a pedagogical choice.

### Outcome 1 (Understand) ← Exercise: Map your activities to your objectives (Analyse)
- **⚠️ EXERCISE EXCEEDS OUTCOME** — The exercise asks learners to *analyse* the connections between activities and objectives, two levels above "explain". **Suggestion:** This exercise primarily serves Outcome 3 (Analyse), so the mismatch with Outcome 1 is minor — it is a secondary link. No change needed here if Outcome 1's verb is levelled up to "apply".

### Outcome 4 (Apply) ← Exercise: Draw a dependency map (Analyse)
- **⚠️ EXERCISE EXCEEDS OUTCOME** — The exercise asks learners to *analyse* dependencies, one level above the outcome's "sequence" (Apply). **Suggestion:** This is a mild mismatch. Analysing dependencies is a natural prerequisite to sequencing, so the exercise is reasonable. Consider levelling up the outcome verb to "analyse" or accepting this as a deliberate stretch.

### Outcome 4 (Apply) ← Exercise: Gradual build-up or productive failure? (Evaluate)
- **⚠️ EXERCISE EXCEEDS OUTCOME** — The exercise asks learners to *evaluate* two approaches and justify a choice, two levels above the outcome's "sequence" (Apply). **Suggestion:** Level up the outcome to include the evaluative component (e.g., "Sequence content so that each piece builds on what came before, and evaluate when productive struggle is more effective than gradual progression"), or simplify the exercise to focus on applying both approaches rather than judging between them.

## Flags for Review

### Orphan Exercises

1. **Write three questions to find out what your learners know** — This exercise supports the "Pitching content at the right level" section but no learning outcome covers needs assessment or audience analysis. **Suggestions:**
   - **Option A (recommended):** Add a new outcome, e.g., "Design a short needs assessment to find out what your learners already know before a session."
   - **Option B:** Fold this into Outcome 5 by broadening it to include audience-level considerations.
   - **Option C:** Accept it as a useful supplementary exercise that enriches the section without needing a dedicated outcome.

2. **Find and evaluate one resource** — This exercise supports the "Using and adapting existing content" section but no outcome covers finding, evaluating, or adapting OER. **Suggestions:**
   - **Option A (recommended):** Add a new outcome, e.g., "Evaluate an existing open educational resource for alignment with your learning objectives and learner level."
   - **Option B:** Accept it as supplementary — OER use is a practical skill the section teaches, but it may not need to be a formal outcome for this chapter.
   - **Option C:** Remove the exercise if the section is meant to be informational rather than skill-building (not recommended — the exercise is valuable).

### Bloom's Mismatches

3. **Outcome 1 verb is too low.** The outcome says "explain" (Understand) but both exercises mapped to it operate at Apply or Analyse. **Suggestion:** Change to "Apply the backward design process to plan a focused training session and explain why it produces more focused training than starting with content." This captures both the conceptual understanding and the practical application the exercises demand.

4. **Outcome 4 verb undersells the exercises.** The outcome says "sequence" (Apply) but the exercises reach Analyse and Evaluate. **Suggestion:** Reword to "Analyse the dependencies between topics in a training session to sequence content effectively, and evaluate when productive struggle is more effective than gradual progression." This matches the actual cognitive demand.

### No Unmet Outcomes

All five outcomes have at least one well-matched exercise. No outcomes are unmet.

### No Vague Verbs

All five outcome verbs are specific Bloom's-aligned verbs (explain, write, identify, sequence, evaluate). None are flagged as vague.
