# Workshop Activity Alignment Report

## Activity 1: The Illusion of Competence
- **What participants do:** Experience the gap between recognition and recall firsthand, then identify teaching practices in their own courses that might create a false sense of learner competence.
- **Activity Bloom's level:** Understand / Analyse (experiencing the concept, then analysing their own teaching for illusion-creating practices)
- **Matched outcome:** Outcome 2 — "Identify teaching practices that may create an illusion of competence in learners."
- **Outcome Bloom's level:** Understand
- **Alignment:** MATCH (after revision). The added step 5 bridges the gap between personal experience and identifying teaching practices, directly serving the outcome. The activity now slightly exceeds the outcome (Analyse vs Understand), which is appropriate for a facilitated workshop.

## Activity 2: Bloom's Taxonomy Remix
- **What participants do:** Write three questions at different cognitive levels about a concept from their own context, evaluate which type is most revealing, then plan how to space these questions across their programme as retrieval practice tools.
- **Activity Bloom's level:** Create (producing original questions) / Evaluate (judging question types) / Apply (planning when to reuse them)
- **Matched outcome:** Outcome 3 — "Describe how spaced repetition and retrieval practice strengthen long-term retention, and design activities that use both."
- **Outcome Bloom's level:** Understand / Create
- **Alignment:** MATCH (after revision). The added step 6 explicitly connects the questions to retrieval practice and spaced repetition, making the link to Outcome 3 direct rather than implicit.

## Activity 3: Spaced Repetition Course Audit
- **What participants do:** Map where key concepts appear across a course programme, identify spacing gaps, and brainstorm ways to revisit under-represented concepts.
- **Activity Bloom's level:** Analyse (mapping and identifying patterns/gaps) / Apply (brainstorming a concrete revisit strategy)
- **Matched outcome:** Outcome 3 — "Describe how spaced repetition and retrieval practice strengthen long-term retention, and design activities that use both."
- **Outcome Bloom's level:** Understand / Create
- **Alignment:** MATCH — The analysis of spacing patterns demonstrates understanding of spaced repetition principles. Step 5 (brainstorming a way to bring a concept back) approaches Create. Complements Activity 2, which covers retrieval practice while this one covers spaced repetition.

## Activity 4: Learning Styles Myth-Busting Debate
- **What participants do:** Argue for/against learning styles, process research evidence, and form evidence-based practical conclusions.
- **Activity Bloom's level:** Evaluate (constructing and assessing arguments, weighing evidence)
- **Matched outcome:** Outcome 4 — "Distinguish between the debunked 'learning styles' model and the evidence-based benefits of multimedia learning."
- **Outcome Bloom's level:** Analyse
- **Alignment:** ACTIVITY EXCEEDS OUTCOME (one level) — The debate format pushes participants to evaluate and justify positions, which goes beyond distinguishing between two models. This is productive stretch in a facilitated workshop setting — no action needed.

## Activity 5: Chunking Challenge
- **What participants do:** Organise a list of items into meaningful groups, compare different groupings across teams, and discuss how experts vs. novices structure knowledge.
- **Activity Bloom's level:** Analyse (comparing organisational structures, drawing connections to expert/novice differences)
- **Matched outcome:** Outcome 1 — "Explain how working memory and long-term memory interact, and apply this understanding to identify gaps in a course where learning may not stick."
- **Outcome Bloom's level:** Understand / Apply
- **Alignment:** MATCH — The activity applies working memory concepts (chunking) and step 5 asks participants to identify places in their own content where chunking could help, covering both the Understand and Apply components.

## Activity 6: Zone of Proximal Development Role-Play
- **What participants do:** Role-play adjusting challenge levels in learning scenarios, observe educator strategies, and discuss signals of misaligned difficulty.
- **Activity Bloom's level:** Apply / Evaluate (applying ZPD in simulated scenarios, evaluating whether adjustments work)
- **Matched outcome:** Outcome 5 — "Apply the zone of proximal development to evaluate whether course exercises are appropriately challenging for the target audience."
- **Outcome Bloom's level:** Apply / Evaluate
- **Alignment:** MATCH

## Unmatched outcomes

- **Outcome 6:** "Evaluate when memorisation is the right starting point and when leading with understanding is more effective." — No dedicated workshop activity. Lightly touched by the optional extension question in Activity 1's facilitator notes. Covered in self-study by "Memorisation vs. understanding in your own course" and "Memorisation audit."

## Summary

- 5 activities aligned (Activities 1, 2, 3, 5, 6)
- 1 activity exceeds its outcome (Activity 4 — by one level, productive stretch)
- 0 activities undershoot their outcome
- 0 activities with no matching outcome
- 1 outcome with no dedicated workshop activity (Outcome 6 — covered by self-study exercises and an optional debrief question)
