# Workshop Activity Alignment Report

## Activity 1: Praise Makeover
- **What participants do:** Classify their praise statements by type (effort, ability, process) and rewrite them to be process-focused.
- **Activity Bloom's level:** Analyse
- **Matched outcome:** Outcome 1 — Apply growth mindset principles when responding to learners in the moment, distinguishing process-focused feedback from effort-only praise.
- **Outcome Bloom's level:** Apply
- **Alignment:** ACTIVITY EXCEEDS OUTCOME (1 level)

Also serves Outcome 7 (Give specific, process-focused feedback) at Apply — exceeds by 1 level. Acceptable stretch in both cases.

## Activity 2: The Productive Failure Lab
- **What participants do:** Design a pre-instruction challenge and plan the follow-up debrief.
- **Activity Bloom's level:** Create
- **Matched outcome:** Outcome 3 — Design activities that use productive failure and desirable difficulties deliberately.
- **Outcome Bloom's level:** Create
- **Alignment:** MATCH

Also serves Outcome 2 (Apply productive struggle understanding) — exceeds by 2 levels, but Outcome 3 is the primary match and is direct.

## Activity 3: Stress Reframe Role-Play
- **What participants do:** Role-play reframing learner stress as a normal, useful part of learning in a simulated training moment.
- **Activity Bloom's level:** Apply
- **Matched outcome:** Outcome 2 — Apply an understanding of productive struggle and the stress-is-enhancing mindset to reframe difficulty as a normal part of the learning process.
- **Outcome Bloom's level:** Apply
- **Alignment:** MATCH

## Activity 4: The Motivation Audit
- **What participants do:** Evaluate their own training sessions against the three SDT needs (autonomy, competence, relatedness) and identify cross-session patterns.
- **Activity Bloom's level:** Evaluate
- **Matched outcome:** Outcome 4 — Identify the psychological factors that sustain motivation and apply them to your own training design.
- **Outcome Bloom's level:** Understand/Apply
- **Alignment:** ACTIVITY EXCEEDS OUTCOME (2 levels)

The gap is large enough to flag. Both the self-study exercise and the workshop activity already ask participants to evaluate. The outcomes map notes this as "acceptable as productive stretch." Consider levelling up Outcome 4 in a future `/harden-outcomes` pass.

## Activity 5: Spot the Threat
- **What participants do:** Classify task instructions as evaluative or learning-focused, then rewrite evaluative ones to reduce stereotype threat.
- **Activity Bloom's level:** Analyse
- **Matched outcome:** Outcome 6 — Adjust how you frame challenges and respond to mistakes in the moment so that learners interpret difficulty as a normal part of learning.
- **Outcome Bloom's level:** Apply
- **Alignment:** ACTIVITY EXCEEDS OUTCOME (1 level)

Also partially serves Outcome 5 (Design structural elements of a psychologically safe environment, Create) — but undershoots that outcome by 2 levels. Activity 6 is the better match for Outcome 5.

## Activity 6: First Mistake Response Plan
- **What participants do:** Write their instinctive response to a learner's public mistake, then craft improved versions for both public and individual scenarios.
- **Activity Bloom's level:** Create
- **Matched outcome:** Outcome 5 — Design the structural elements of a psychologically safe learning environment.
- **Outcome Bloom's level:** Create
- **Alignment:** MATCH

Also serves Outcome 6 (Adjust framing and mistake response, Apply) — exceeds by 2 levels, but acceptable since Activity 5 also covers Outcome 6 at a closer level.

## Activity 7: Feedback Rewrite
- **What participants do:** Classify their feedback by focus (outcome, person, process) and rewrite it to name specific strategies and connect to progress.
- **Activity Bloom's level:** Analyse
- **Matched outcome:** Outcome 7 — Give specific, process-focused feedback on learner work that highlights progress and strategies used.
- **Outcome Bloom's level:** Apply
- **Alignment:** ACTIVITY EXCEEDS OUTCOME (1 level)

Acceptable stretch. Mirrors the classify-then-rewrite structure of Activity 1 but targets feedback on completed work rather than in-the-moment praise.
