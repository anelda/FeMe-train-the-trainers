## Workshop Activity Alignment Report

### Activity 1: The Bad Assessment Autopsy
- **What participants do:** Analyse a bad assessment to identify what it actually measured versus what it should have measured, and propose one fix.
- **Activity Bloom's level:** Analyse
- **Matched outcome:** Outcome 2 — "Distinguish between signal and noise in assessment results, and adjust your design to reduce noise."
- **Outcome Bloom's level:** Analyse / Apply
- **Alignment:** MATCH

### Activity 2: Signal or Noise?
- **What participants do:** Identify signal and noise in assessment scenarios and propose a design change to reduce the noise.
- **Activity Bloom's level:** Analyse / Apply
- **Matched outcome:** Outcome 2 — "Distinguish between signal and noise in assessment results, and adjust your design to reduce noise."
- **Outcome Bloom's level:** Analyse / Apply
- **Alignment:** MATCH

### Activity 3: Recognition vs. Recall — Live Experiment
- **What participants do:** Experience two question formats first-hand, compare results, and discuss what each format actually proves and when each is appropriate.
- **Activity Bloom's level:** Evaluate
- **Matched outcome:** Outcome 4 — "Evaluate the difference between recall and recognition, and justify your choice of assessment format."
- **Outcome Bloom's level:** Evaluate
- **Alignment:** MATCH

### Activity 4: Assessment Design Sprint
- **What participants do:** Design three different assessments for a real topic (at least one non-test format), articulate what each reveals, and receive peer feedback.
- **Activity Bloom's level:** Create
- **Matched outcome:** Outcome 1 — "Design assessments that reveal where learners are struggling, rather than simply ranking them."
- **Outcome Bloom's level:** Create
- **Alignment:** MATCH

### Activity 5: Defuse the Anxiety — Role Play
- **What participants do:** Improvise a 30-second verbal assessment introduction, respond to sceptical learner pushback, collect effective phrases, then draft a written 2–3 sentence introduction they'd actually use.
- **Activity Bloom's level:** Create (the written draft raises it from Apply to Create)
- **Matched outcome:** Outcome 5 — "Draft a transparent assessment introduction that reduces test anxiety, drawing on evidence about how anxiety affects learner performance."
- **Outcome Bloom's level:** Create
- **Alignment:** MATCH (after adding the drafting step)

### Activity 6: Where Do Your Checkpoints Go?
- **What participants do:** Map their training programme timeline, identify where assessment currently happens, and plan two additional low-stakes checkpoint moments with a partner.
- **Activity Bloom's level:** Create (planning new assessment points)
- **Matched outcome:** Outcome 3 — "Plan frequent, low-stakes assessment points across a training programme, based on evidence that spacing supports learning more effectively than a single high-stakes exam."
- **Outcome Bloom's level:** Create
- **Alignment:** MATCH

None — all five outcomes now have at least one corresponding workshop activity.
